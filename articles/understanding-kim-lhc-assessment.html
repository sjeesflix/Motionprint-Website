The KIM-LHC (Key Indicator Method — Lifting, Holding, Carrying) is a screening tool for evaluating the physical load of manual handling tasks. It was developed by the German Federal Institute for Occupational Safety and Health (BAuA). Motionprint Ergo implements it in full — automatically extracting body posture, load handling, and biomechanical variables from motion capture data and computing the risk score for every lifting moment in your recording. This article explains how the method works, what each scoring component represents, and how to interpret your results.

1 Overview

KIM-LHC produces a single End Score (also called the risk score) for each lifting moment. The End Score determines the risk level across four categories:

End Score Risk Level Interpretation
< 20 Low risk Physical load is low. Health consequences from physical overload are unlikely.
20 – 50 Slightly increased risk Physical overload is possible for less resilient persons. Workplace redesign for this group may be helpful.
50 – 100 Substantially increased risk Physical overload is also possible for normally resilient persons. Workplace redesign is recommended.
≥ 100 High risk Physical overload is likely. Workplace redesign is necessary.
Automated measurement

Motionprint Ergo derives body posture classification, trunk bending/twisting, hand position, load handling asymmetry, and hand-arm movement automatically from your motion capture data — no manual posture observation required.

2 The End Score Formula

The End Score is calculated by multiplying the Total Rating Points by the Time Rating:

End Score End Score = Total Rating Points × Time Rating

Total Rating Points is itself the sum of five individual scoring components:

Total Rating Points Load Rating + Load Handling + Total Body Score + Unfavorable Working Conditions + Work Organization

Each component contributes points that reflect a specific aspect of physical workload. The Time Rating then acts as a multiplier that scales the total based on how frequently the task is repeated during the work shift. A high Total Rating Points value at low frequency can still produce an acceptable End Score, and vice versa — frequency is a critical part of the overall assessment.

Customizable in settings

The load rating tables can be fully customized in the KIM-LHC report settings. You can also disable the 0 – 3 kg weight category if it is not relevant to your assessments. Custom tables are stored per gender and persist across sessions.

4 Load Handling

Load Handling assesses how symmetrically the load is held. Motionprint Ergo determines this automatically by measuring the lateral and vertical asymmetry between the left and right hand positions relative to the pelvis. A threshold of 100 mm is applied for both horizontal and vertical asymmetry.

Points Condition
0 Load is handled with both hands and symmetrically
2 Load is handled temporarily with one hand and/or asymmetrically, uneven load distribution
4 Load is handled predominantly with one hand or with an unstable load centre

Color-coded risk indication for Load Handling: Green = 0  ·  Yellow = 2  ·  Red = 4.

5 Body Posture Score

The body posture score is determined from the combination of the worker's start posture (at the beginning of the lifting moment) and finish posture (at the end). Motionprint Ergo classifies these automatically from the motion capture data using six biomechanical features: trunk flexion, shoulder flexion, elbow flexion, knee flexion, knee height, and hand height. Each feature is weighted and compared against reference ranges to identify the best-matching posture class.

Posture classes

Ten posture classes are defined, ranging from fully upright to highly strained positions:

  1. Standing straight
  2. Slightly bent back
  3. Medium bent back
  4. Bended back
  5. Max bended back
  6. Lifting arms (hands above shoulder)
  7. Squat with straight back
  8. Squat low
  9. On knees (upright)
  10. Sitting on knees

Combination score

The start→finish posture combination produces a body posture score from 0 to 20 points. The score depends on how ergonomically demanding both postures are and how different they are from each other:

Automated posture detection

Manual KIM-LHC assessments require the assessor to visually judge the start and finish posture. Motionprint Ergo identifies these automatically from joint angles in your motion capture data, removing observer bias and eliminating the need for a trained assessor to be present during the task.

6 Additional Points (Body Posture)

On top of the base body posture score, additional points are added for specific posture conditions detected during the lifting moment. The total of all additional points is capped at 6. The Total Body Score is therefore:

Total Body Score Body Posture Score + min(Additional Points, 6)

Four conditions are evaluated, all measured automatically from motion capture data:

Twisting / Lateral bending

Detects whether the trunk is significantly rotated or laterally bent during the lift. The threshold is 20° for both lateral bending and trunk twisting.

7 Unfavorable Working Conditions

Six environmental and task condition factors contribute additional points to the assessment. These reflect conditions that increase musculoskeletal strain beyond what load, posture, and frequency alone capture. Five of the six factors are entered manually on the input page; hand/arm movement is the one factor derived automatically from motion capture data.

Factor Scoring Source
Hand/arm movement (wrist deviations) 0, 1, or 2 — based on wrist flexion >75°, extension >60°, ulnar deviation >30°, radial deviation >15°, pronation >60°, supination >60°, using the same 5% / 25% frame thresholds as additional points Derived automatically from motion capture
Force transfer / force application 0, 1, or 2 Manual entry
Adverse ambient conditions 0 or 1 Manual entry
Spatial conditions 0, 1, or 2 Manual entry
Difficulties holding / carrying 0, 2, or 5 Manual entry
Impairing clothing 0 or 1 Manual entry

The Unfavorable Conditions Score is the sum of all six factors.

Color-coded risk indication for Unfavorable Working Conditions: Green ≤ 4  ·  Yellow 5 – 9  ·  Red ≥ 10.

9 Time Rating

The Time Rating is a multiplier between 1 and 10 that reflects how frequently the lifting task is performed during a full work shift, expressed in cycles per day. In Motionprint Ergo, frequency is calculated automatically: the user enters the total working hours or minutes, and the app derives the cycle duration from the trimmed recording length and the number of defined lifting moments.

The Time Rating is the same for all moments in the assessment — it reflects the overall shift workload, not the frequency of individual lifting moments. The cycle duration is computed from the total recording length divided by the number of defined moments.

10 Multi-Moment Assessments

When a worker performs more than one distinct lifting movement within a recording, Motionprint Ergo supports defining multiple lifting moments. Each moment has its own frame range and load weight, and is scored independently — producing its own Load Rating, Load Handling, Total Body Score, Unfavorable Working Conditions, Work Organization, and End Score.

The results page provides a moment selector to switch between individual moment results. When multiple moments exist, the app also computes an averaged overview that aggregates scores across all moments, giving a holistic view of the overall task demand for the full recording.

In Motionprint Ergo

The KIM-LHC method does not define a formal composite index formula for multi-task aggregation. Moment scores are independent and an averaged overview is provided across all defined moments.

11 Implementation in Motionprint Ergo

Motionprint Ergo implements the full KIM-LHC method in a dedicated processing pipeline that applies BAuA posture thresholds and scoring criteria to the motion capture data frame by frame, across all defined lifting moments.

Calculation workflow

1
Input data

Body posture, trunk angles, hand positions, wrist angles, and load handling asymmetry are extracted automatically from motion capture data. Manual inputs — load weight category, working conditions, work organization, working hours, and gender — are entered in the assessment setup panel and passed to the processing pipeline.

2
Score computation

Each scoring component is computed per lifting moment. The body posture is classified using weighted joint angle matching against the 10 reference postures. Additional points for twisting, hand distance, and hand height are determined from frame-by-frame analysis using percentage-of-frames thresholds.

3
End Score calculation

All component scores are summed into Total Rating Points, then multiplied by the Time Rating to produce the End Score. For multi-moment assessments, individual scores and an averaged overview are computed across all defined moments.

4
Report output

Results are presented across three report tabs. Score overview shows a visual summary of all components with color-coded risk indicators for each: Load Rating, Load Handling, Total Body Score, Unfavorable Working Conditions, Work Organization, and the final End Score. In-depth analysis lets you explore per-frame score graphs, critical moments, and a synchronized 3D viewer to see exactly which frames and postures are driving the score. Findings (if enabled in settings) provides editable sections for documenting observations — the same structure as in the NIOSH report.
