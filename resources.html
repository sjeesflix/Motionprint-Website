Built on peer-reviewed science

Motionprint Ergo's assessment methods are grounded in validated, internationally recognised ergonomic research.

Reference study — NIOSH
Waters, T.R. et al. (1993) — Revised NIOSH Equation for the Design and Evaluation of Manual Lifting Tasks

The foundational study behind the NIOSH Lifting Equation used in Motionprint Ergo. Establishes the Recommended Weight Limit and Lifting Index calculation methodology.

Reference study — KIM
BAuA — Key Indicator Method for Lifting, Holding and Carrying (KIM-LHC)

The German Federal Institute for Occupational Safety and Health's standardised method for assessing musculoskeletal loads in manual handling tasks.

Frequently asked questions

Which Xsens suits and software are supported?

Motionprint Ergo currently supports .mvnx files exported from MVN Studio (any version). This covers all Xsens Awinda and Link suit configurations. Support for the Xsens SDK and direct streaming is on the roadmap.

What operating systems does Motionprint Ergo run on?

Motionprint Ergo is currently a Windows desktop application. A macOS and cloud version are planned for a future release.

How many users are included in the subscription?

The €2,500/year subscription is an organisation licence — it covers your team. Contact us if you need a multi-site arrangement or have questions about specific use cases.

Are new assessment methods included in my subscription?

Yes. When new methods (RULA, REBA, OCRA, etc.) are added to Motionprint Ergo, they are automatically available to all active subscribers at no extra cost.

Can I request a specific assessment method or feature?

Absolutely. Use the contact form to submit feature requests or method suggestions — we actively use customer feedback to prioritise the roadmap.
